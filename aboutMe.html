About Me

Get to know me.

I was born and raised in the suburbs of Atlanta, Georgia. I live with my mom, dad, and younger sister. We also have a few furry friends that keep us company.

Hello! My name is Mary Sauls. I am an undergrad student at the Georgia Institute of Technology majoring in Computational Media. I attended the University of Alabama as a Computer Science major for a year before transferring. Throughout the years, I have had a lot of exposure to programming. When I was younger, I created a mod for Minecraft and began venturing into robotics with a Lego Mindstorms Robotics kit. As I got into Middle School and High School, I was able to join my school's FIRST Robotics teams. Although I mainly wanted to do programming, it gave me a lot of exposure other things like mechanical engineering, design, safety, and buisness. The FIRST Robotics program was really a great way for me to explore more aspects in an engineering field other than what I had already been exposed to, as well as introducing me to other students from other schools all over the state with similar interests as me. Because of the COVID-19 pandemic, I was unfortunately unable to complete my senior year on the team, as everyone went into quarantine. Starting college the next year by myself in a new place was culture shock enough; however, online classes and a continued pandemic made the situation worse. I was unable to join any clubs or really meet any new people at all. It really put a damper on trying to get involved with robotics, but I was still able to work on my skills in a C course I was taking. However, I learned that I didn't just want to sit at a desk and code all day. I wanted to be creative and help design things, so I transferred to Georgia Tech as a Computational Media major, where I can put both of those aspects together and try to learn more about User Interface and Experience.

And that brings me to today, thank you for checking me out! If you would like a copy of my resume, please fill out your information in "Contact Me" and indicate so. I look forward to hearing from you!
