My Projects

Haiku Twitter Bot

This was a group project for LMC2700 Introduction to Computational Media. For this project, a group of students had to create a functioning Twitter Bot. The bot our group created retweets a tweet from #mediaarts every hour. It takes a tweet and counts the syllables in the sentence. If it doesn't have seventeen syllables, it moves to the next one. When a tweet has the right amount of syllables, it parses the tweet into the 5/7/5 format.

A-Frame 3D Image

Another project for the LMC2700 course was replicating an image in A-Frame. We had to take a 2D image and convert it to a 3D scene. Essentially, we were to make paintings into sculptures. As I was new to A-Frame, I chose "Composition of Red, Yellow, and Blue" by Piet Mondrian. The image above is the oil painting, and the link will take you to the A-Frame.

Froggy Hop

For my final project in LMC2700 Introduction to Computational Media, I was tasked with creating a video game using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. My partner and I decieded to go with a game based off of Sega's Frogger. Instead of an infinite world with cars and logs, we decided to make it a timed game with logs and lily pads. The goal is to use the arrow keys to hop across the body of water on the moving logs and stationary lilypads. I coded this project, while my partner did the write up and formal documentation. It is only a prototype; however, I really enjoyed this project and hope to update it in the future.
